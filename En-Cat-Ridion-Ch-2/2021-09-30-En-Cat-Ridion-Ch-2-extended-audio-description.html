Chapter 2
Desire to watch a television show demands space to watch that television show.
Aversion to being interrupted provokes annoyance when the cat parks his fuzzy but in front of the screen.
She who fails to catch her television show is disappointed,
And she who screen is persistently blocked by the cat is wretched.
If, then, you want only those things that are in your control, you will not care if the cat interrupted your television show.
But if you want to have an uninterrupted viewing experience, or never have the flu, or just have the curly parts of the cat, you will be miserable.
But if you want uninterrupted television experience,
or to avoid getting clawed by the cat until you feed him,
or in general have life go the way you want,
he's not gonna let you have a very fun time.
Remove the habit of wanting the cat to be anything but a cat,
For that matter, try and control what you want, or you might end up with him meowing for hours to try and get me to feed him an extra serving.
